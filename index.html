I am a PhD candidate in the field of management and organization studies at EM Lyon Business School, France.

I explore the influence of culture and meaning structures on organizations and their audiences. Adopting a cultural perspective, I use quantitative text analysis and econometric models to examine phenomena such as institutional persistence and social evaluation. My empirical settings include social housing and cultural experiences — for example, how public housing has persisted as an institution for nearly a century in Vienna, using dynamic modeling of discourse in a coauthored paper published in Organization Studies.

About

About me

I am an organizational researcher completing my PhD at EM Lyon Business School (defense scheduled for July 2026), where I teach courses on strategy, sustainability, and microeconomics in the PGE program. In my research, I use machine learning techniques such as word embeddings and structural/dynamic topic modeling to identify patterns in large corpora. I am a part of the Strategy & Organization (STORM) and (InvEnt) research centers at EM Lyon.

Previously, I worked as a research analyst at École normale supérieure (ENS) and Edelman Intelligence in Paris. I studied economics at Allameh Tabatabai University (BS) and the University of Amsterdam (MSc), as well as innovation and management at SKEMA Business School (MSc).

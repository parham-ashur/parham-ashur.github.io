I study social evaluation, responsible governance, and social sustainability through a cultural lens.

I am an organizational researcher with a PhD in management from EM Lyon Business School. Using quantitative text analysis and econometric models, I examine how culture and meaning structures shape organizations and the audiences that judge them — such as how Vienna’s public housing endured for a century, in a coauthored paper published in Organization Studies.

About me

I recently completed my PhD at EM Lyon Business School, where I have taught strategy, sustainability, and microeconomics in the PGE program. I use machine learning methods like word embeddings and structural/dynamic topic modeling to find patterns in large text corpora. I’m part of the Strategy & Organization (STORM) and Entrepreneurship and Innovation (InvEnt) research centers at EM Lyon.

Earlier, I was a research analyst at École normale supérieure and Edelman Intelligence in Paris. I studied economics at Allameh Tabatabai University (BS) and the University of Amsterdam (MSc), and innovation and management at SKEMA Business School (MSc).

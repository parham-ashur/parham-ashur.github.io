I am a PhD candidate in the field of management and organization studies at EM Lyon Business School, France.

I examine the influence of culture and meaning structures on organizations and their audiences across different levels. Using both quantitative and qualitative methods, I apply a cultural perspective to investigate phenomena like institutional persistence and social evaluation. My work spans various empirical contexts, from social housing to creative industry, including areas such as restaurants, books, and movies.

About me

I am an organizational researcher and a PhD candidate at EM Lyon Business School, where I teach Strategy and Microeconomics in the PGE program. In my research, I use machine learning techniques such as word embeddings and structural/dynamic topic modeling to identify patterns in large corpora. I am a part of the Strategy & Organization (STORM) and (InvEnt) research centers at EM Lyon.

Previously, I worked as a research analyst at École normale supérieure (ENS) and Edelman Intelligence in Paris. I studied economics at Allameh Tabataba'i University (BA) and the University of Amsterdam (MSc), as well as innovation and management at SKEMA Business School (MSc).

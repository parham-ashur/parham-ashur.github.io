Data Visualization | Eye-tracking Data Stream

March 2019

In this project, I replicate a paper [Here] that I'm working on an extension to it. In this paper, authors investigated whether children create predictions about the distribution of syntactic categories in a new environment, and draw on these to acquire word meanings. They found that children who heard examples of la petite + familiar verbs were more likely to infer that a novel word produced after la petite referred to an action, than children who heard la petite + familiar nouns sentences. This suggests that children did not only rapidly adapt their expectation to encounter a specific syntactic category, but also utilise their adapted predictions to learn. Children from the verb-condition (M = 0.602, SD = 0.117), are significantly more likely to look at the action video than children from the noun-condition (M = 0.482, SD = 0.139), suggesting that children from the verb-condition were more likely to interpret the novel word as referring to the action than children in the noun-condition (Figure.1).

Data Visualization | Paris Weather in 2018

March 2019

In this project, I compared graphically Paris average daily temperature (2018) to the aggregated average temperature data since 1995.



Data is obtained from the University of Dayton's Average Daily Temperature Archive. This project is a reproduction of a work by Brad Boehmke in a blog [link] on weather visualization. [for R codes follow the link to my Github]
